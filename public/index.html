RULES

The Custom Definitive Mahjong Rules

Optimized for 2, 3, or 4 players using the full 144-tile deck. A fast-paced "Free-for-All Chow" house rule and a strict, high-stakes chip economy.

1. The Hierarchy of Time

  • The Turn — draw (or claim a discard), then discard.
  • The Hand — one deal, from opening tiles to someone calling "Mahjong!" (or the wall running out). Chips settle immediately at the end of every hand.
  • The Round — a block of play under one Prevailing Wind, complete once the dealer role has rotated through every active seat.
  • The Game — a full match of 4 rounds: East, South, West, North.

2. Components

All 144 tiles: Suits (108) — Dots, Bamboos, Characters, 1-9 x4 each. Honors (28) — 4 Winds, 3 Dragons x4 each. Bonus (8) — 4 Flowers, 4 Seasons.

Starting chips: 500/player at a full 4-player table; 1,000/player with only 2 or 3 (chips concentrate faster with fewer players, so everyone gets a bigger buffer).

3. Setup & Dealing

Highest roll is East (the dealer); seats run East → South (right) → West (across) → North (left). All 4 walls are built regardless of player count, so the full tile pool always stays in play — empty seats just don't get dealt in. Flowers/Seasons drawn are flipped up immediately and replaced from the back of the wall.

4. Melds

MeldDescriptionExample
Chow3 consecutive tiles, same suit4, 5, 6 of Bamboos
Pung3 identical tilesThree White Dragons
Kong4 identical tiles (draws a replacement tile)Four 9 of Dots
Pair (the Eye)2 identical tilesTwo East Winds

A winning hand is 4 melds + 1 pair. Melds built by claiming a discard are laid face-up ("exposed") and can't be broken apart afterward.

5. Turn Flow & Free-for-All Claims

Any player may instantly claim the most recent discard if it completes a meld — turn order resumes from the claimer's seat. Priority: Mahjong beats everything, then Pung/Kong, then Chow — and under this table's house rule, a Chow may be claimed from any discarder, not just the player to your left.

6. Scoring — Fan → Chips

Total your Fan, then convert to a base chip value via exponential doubling (0 fan = 1 chip, 1 = 2, 2 = 4, 3 = 8, 4 = 16, 5 = 32, 6+/Limit Hand = 64):

  • Concealed Hand — 1 fan
  • Self-Draw / Zimo — 1 fan
  • No Chows (all Pungs/Kongs + a pair) — 1 fan
  • Matching Wind (seat or round) — 1 fan
  • Dragon Pung — 1 fan
  • Correct Flower/Season (matches your seat number) — 1 fan
  • All Chows — 2 fan
  • One Suit + Honors — 3 fan
  • All Pungs — 3 fan
  • Pure One Suit — 6 fan
  • Thirteen Orphans — Limit Hand (capped at 64 chips)

Named special hands from the illustrated catalog (see the Special Hands tab) score on their own fixed tier instead — Half Limit (32), Limit (64), Middle Limit (96), or Double Limit (128) chips — rather than being fan-counted.

7. Payouts

Discard win: only the discarder pays the base value. Self-draw win: every other active player pays the base value, plus a flat +1 self-draw bonus each. Dealer factor: if the dealer wins, their payout doubles; if a non-dealer wins, the dealer (as a payer) pays double. A dealer's win streak multiplies their payout further on each consecutive win.

8. Round Progression & Bankruptcy

The dealer keeps their seat after a win or a draw (their win streak grows on a win, resets on losing the deal). Once the deal rotates through every active seat, the round ends and the Prevailing Wind advances. If a player can't pay their debt at the end of a hand and drops to 0 or below, the match ends immediately — remaining players are ranked by chip count, and the bankrupt player takes last place.
